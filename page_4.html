Ancient Greece

Rise

Ancient Greece emerged around the 8th century BCE, characterized by city-states (polis) such as Athens, Sparta, and Corinth. Greek civilization made significant contributions to philosophy, democracy, art, architecture, and literature.

Peak

The peak of Ancient Greece occurred during the Classical Period (5thâ€“4th centuries BCE), marked by the Golden Age of Athens under the leadership of Pericles. This era saw the flourishing of democracy, philosophy (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle), and the arts (drama, sculpture, architecture).

Fall

Greece fell under the control of the Macedonian Empire led by Alexander the Great in the 4th century BCE. Following Alexander's death, Greece was divided among his generals, leading to the Hellenistic Period. Eventually, Greece fell under Roman domination by the 2nd century BCE.
