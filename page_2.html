Mesopotamia

Rise

Mesopotamia, located in the fertile crescent between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, saw the rise of several ancient civilizations, including Sumer, Akkad, Assyria, and Babylon. The invention of writing (cuneiform) and the development of complex societies characterized this region's rise.

Peak

The peak of Mesopotamian civilization occurred during the Akkadian Empire (c. 2334–2154 BCE) and the Babylonian Empire (c. 18th–6th centuries BCE). Notable achievements include the Code of Hammurabi, the Hanging Gardens of Babylon, and advances in mathematics and astronomy.

Fall

Mesopotamia experienced periods of conquest and decline due to invasions by neighboring powers, including the Hittites, Assyrians, and Persians. The region eventually fell under the control of the Persian Empire and later succumbed to the conquests of Alexander the Great.
