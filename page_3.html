Ancient Rome

Rise

Rome was founded in 753 BCE and gradually expanded its influence over the Italian Peninsula. Rome's rise to power was characterized by the establishment of a republic, military conquests, and the assimilation of conquered peoples.

Peak

The Roman Republic (509–27 BCE) and later the Roman Empire (27 BCE–476 CE) represented the peak of Roman civilization. During this time, Rome became a dominant political, military, and cultural force in the Mediterranean world, spreading its influence through conquest, trade, and infrastructure projects.

Fall

The fall of the Western Roman Empire occurred in 476 CE due to a combination of internal decay, external invasions (by barbarian tribes such as the Visigoths and Vandals), economic decline, and political instability. The Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine Empire) continued to thrive until the fall of Constantinople in 1453 CE.
